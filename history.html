History of Black Angus Cattle

From Scotland to the world

Home History Production

Black Angus cattle originated in northeastern Scotland during the early 1800s. Local farmers selectively bred native cattle to improve meat quality, fertility, and durability.

The breed was first imported to the United States in 1873, where it rapidly gained popularity due to its polled genetics and superior beef.

Over time, selective breeding has increased size, growth efficiency, marbling, and consistency.
