Purpose

Black Angus cattle are raised primarily for beef production. They are internationally recognized for producing meat with high levels of marbling, which contributes to tenderness, juiciness, and flavor. Angus genetics are widely used in both purebred and crossbreeding programs to improve carcass quality.

Strengths

  • High carcass quality and marbling
  • Efficient feed conversion and growth performance
  • Naturally polled (hornless) genetics
  • Strong consumer demand and premium beef markets
  • Well-documented genetic records and performance data

Weaknesses

  • Lower heat tolerance compared to some tropical breeds
  • Dark coat color absorbs more heat in hot climates
  • Requires careful genetic management to avoid inherited conditions

Information Sources: Certified Angus Beef; American Angus Association; USDA; Oklahoma State University Animal Science
